Problems To Be Solved:

  • Americans are losing their lives.
  • Civil Liberties are being denied.
  • Economic opportunity in this country is quickly disappearing.

What is the issue that Americans face today? The issue is that the country and its people are being ravaged, and people are losing their lives, freedom, and ability to pursue happiness via the American Dream, which is dying before our eyes. Despite the lies told by politicians about how the country is becoming Great Again, our countrymen and countrywomen are dying deaths of desperation and anxiety. These problems need to be addressed in every way.

Solutions

Making healthcare affordable and investing in telehealth and mental health resources
Encouraging gun ownership, removing economic barriers to gun ownership, and removing bans on accessories and types of firearms
Legalizing marijuana
Decriminalizing the possession of all other drugs, and investing in safe-usage sites
Demilitarizing the police
Cutting back on military investment and ending involvement in foreign affairs
Open borders barring the sick and criminal
Protecting internet privacy rights and making data personal property
Encouraging education in the trades
Making college affordable and removing bureaucracy in educational institutions
A universal basic income of $1,000 or $2,000 a month 

These solutions help to end multiple things, and start a few others. They end the dangerous road towards an oppressive police state without any way for citizens to defend themself in the US. They end the endless wars that have destroyed the lives of so many and wasted taxpayer money. They end the punitive, unnecessary laws that ruin the futures of entire communities, the digital surveillance not unlike life in 1984, and bureaucracies that hurt those seeking education.

They start the US down a road to renaissance. They turn the country into one not divided by bipartisan politics, but one focused on solving problems. In particular, a universal basic income would transform the economy and revolutionize our lives in a time of ever-increasing automation. It helps those that are going to be economically insecure as a result of technological job loss and gives them money for basic resources. It turns the economy from one based on the fear of starvation to one based on working out of want and joy. It gives value to the work of caregivers, stay-at-home parents, artists, and volunteers, and it improves the lives of families and children at home and at school by giving them a more stable financial situation.
